    Chef Cho (formerly Liu He Snacks in San Jose) takes over the space of Q Cafe in Fremont.

    I heard that Q Cafe, which served Dim Sum, recently shut down and was taken over by a Taiwanese tapas style restaurant.  I went to try it out on a Thursday afternoon (12:30ish), and the place was packed!  I put my name on the waiting list, but after 15 minutes, I decided to try it out the next day -- only earlier.  Good sign that the place was busy.

    So I returned Friday a little after 11 -- I was the first guest.  I was seated, and the waitress serving me was friendly and helpful.  She explained that it was now a Taiwanese restaurant which opened 3 weeks ago.

    I went with the Yelp suggestions and ordered the Sticky Rice in a barrel, Oyster Pancakes, Taiwanese Hamburger (Gua Bao), and Dry Sautéed String Beans.

    As my dishes came out in relatively rapid succession, people started coming in.  By the time I left, the place was packed again.

    The food was good.  Since I had never been to the San Jose location, I don't know how it was there.  But based on how my food tasted and the bustling crowds coming to have lunch, I'd say this place is worth checking out.  I'll be back to explore more of their menu items.
