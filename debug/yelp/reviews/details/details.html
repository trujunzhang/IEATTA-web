    The restaurant is pretty, with a dramatic entrance, and as others have noted, much quieter upstairs where the ceilings are lower and there is an adjacent lighted courtyard.

    We brought our own bottle of fine wine, which turned out to be a good idea.  Although the corkage is $40, it's hard to find anything on the list for less than $100.  The wine markup is huge.

    The service, and the food, were weird.  We ordered three appetizers, making clear to the server that some in our group could not eat some of them due to dietary restrictions.  Nonetheless, the server brought them seriatim, leaving some of our group feeling rather underfed.  The olives were good; the kanachi was overwhelmingly hot.

    For the entree, our group chose the short ribs, not just because of the past reviews, but also because the server recommended them above anything else on the menu.  The menu warns that they take 30 minutes to prepare, but in fact we waited for about 90 minutes, most of which was without any food on the table.  After an hour, we inquired about the status, and were told the ribs were "being flayed", but apparently just taking the meat off the bones took 30 minutes.  

    When we got them -- disaster.  They were about 70% fat,  which was marbled into the scant meat, so that unless you have a cholesterol rating of 90, the dish is a death knell.  (The server conceded that 50% fat is normal.)  This was unlike any short rib preparation any of our group had ever seen.  None of us could eat it.  It was, in a word, disgusting.  We mentioned our displeasure to the server but no adjustment to the bill was made except to waive corkage.

    To be fair, some of the accompaniments to the short ribs were excellent, including the kale, the potatoes, and the couscous.  The presentation was also lovely.  

    But on the whole, our group's experience at this restaurant was very unsatisfactory.  Given the price point, there is no way any of us would return.
